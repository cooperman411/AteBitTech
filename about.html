About this site

Ate Bit Tech is a curated directory of vintage and retro computing resources. Everything from complete museums to one-person restoration blogs, reference archives to modern FPGA projects. If it's useful, interesting, or historically important to the retro computing community, it belongs here.

The site started as a personal collection of links. There was no single place to discover what was out there, so this is an attempt to create one. Entries are reviewed and added by hand to keep the signal high and the noise low.

The directory is not comprehensive. There are thousands of sites out there. This is a curated corner of the collection. If something good is missing, send a note.

The Price Guide

The Price Guide uses eBay's public API to show current listings and recent sold prices for vintage computing items. It's designed to help people figure out what things are actually worth before buying or selling. Select a brand, pick a category, and enter a search term for the most accurate pricing. The comparisons are based on actual eBay sold data, not guesses.

Some links in the Price Guide are eBay Partner Network affiliate links. If you buy something after clicking one, the site may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you.

Why this exists

The retro computing community is one of the best corners of the internet. People restoring old hardware, documenting obscure systems, writing new software for 40-year-old machines. This site is a way of contributing to that community.
